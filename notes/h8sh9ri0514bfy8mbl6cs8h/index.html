Splitting a distribution into a mixture with the same mean and variance

Let the distribution have mean μ\muμ and variance σ2\sigma^2σ2. This can be done by using the equation for the mixture in the following way:

  1. Take as parameters the number of mixtures to split into LLL
  2. As another parameter the target variance of each component in the mixtures σcomp2\sigma_{comp}^2σcomp2​.
  3. Set the target mean of the means of the mixture be the original distribution mean μ\muμ. That would make the mean of the mixture equal to the mean of the original distribution.
  4. Then we need same for the variance. We will achieve it by taking the variance of the means in the mixture to be: σ2=σcomp2+Var(means)\sigma^2 = \sigma_{comp}^2 + Var(means)σ2=σcomp2​+Var(means). This directly gives us the variance of the means, and we can just sample from them and sort.
