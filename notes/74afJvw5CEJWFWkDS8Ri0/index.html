Independence

We say the random variables (R,A){\textstyle (R,A)}(R,A) satisfy independence if the sensitive characteristics AAA are statistically independent to the prediction RRR, and we write R⊥AR\bot AR⊥A.

We can also express this notion with the following formula:

P(R=r∣A=a)=P(R=r∣A=b)∀r∈R∀a,b∈A{P(R=r|A=a)=P(R=r|A=b)\quad \forall r\in R\quad \forall a,b\in A}P(R=r∣A=a)=P(R=r∣A=b)∀r∈R∀a,b∈A for RRR-result, AAA -sensitive characteristic.

This means that the probability of being classified by the algorithm in each of the groups is equal for two individuals with different sensitive characteristics. I think this is roughly equivalent of the 'same error for all groups'.
